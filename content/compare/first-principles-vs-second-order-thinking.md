## First Principles Thinking

Break any problem down to its fundamental truths, then build your reasoning up from there.

[Read the full model →](/models/first-principles/)

## Second-Order Thinking

Consider not just the immediate consequences of a decision, but the consequences of those consequences.

[Read the full model →](/models/second-order-thinking/)

## What's the difference?

First Principles strips a problem down to fundamental truths and rebuilds from scratch. Second-Order Thinking keeps the existing framework but traces consequences further — asking 'and then what?' beyond the obvious first effects.

## When to use each

Use First Principles when you suspect the conventional approach is fundamentally wrong and needs to be rebuilt from the ground up. Use Second-Order Thinking when the approach is sound but you need to anticipate cascading consequences before committing.

## In practice

Elon Musk used First Principles to rethink rocket costs from raw materials up. Howard Marks uses Second-Order Thinking to find investments that the market has mispriced by thinking one step further than the consensus.

## They work together

These models aren't competitors — they're complements. The best thinkers use both, selecting the right tool for the right situation. First Principles Thinking and Second-Order Thinking often appear together in [Model Chains](/learn/model-chains/), where the output of one feeds into the other.
