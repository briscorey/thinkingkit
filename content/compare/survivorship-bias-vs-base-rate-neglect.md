## Survivorship Bias

We study the winners and forget the losers — which distorts our understanding of what actually causes success.

[Read the full model →](/models/survivorship-bias/)

## Base Rate Neglect

We ignore how common or rare something is in general, and focus too much on the specific case in front of us.

[Read the full model →](/models/base-rate-neglect/)

## What's the difference?

Survivorship Bias means you're only seeing the winners and missing the losers. Base Rate Neglect means you're ignoring how common or rare the outcome is in general. Both involve missing data, but different kinds: survivorship hides who failed; base rate neglect hides how many tried.

## When to use each

Use Survivorship Bias awareness when learning from success stories — ask 'who tried the same thing and failed?' Use Base Rate awareness before making predictions — ask 'how common is this outcome in general?'

## They work together

These models aren't competitors — they're complements. The best thinkers use both.
