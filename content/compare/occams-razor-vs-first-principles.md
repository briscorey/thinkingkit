## Occam's Razor

The simplest explanation that fits the evidence is usually the correct one.

[Read the full model →](/models/occams-razor/)

## First Principles Thinking

Break any problem down to its fundamental truths, then build your reasoning up from there.

[Read the full model →](/models/first-principles/)

## What's the difference?

Occam's Razor starts with existing explanations and prefers the simplest one. First Principles ignores existing explanations entirely and rebuilds understanding from fundamental truths. Occam's Razor is a filter for choosing between theories. First Principles is a method for generating new theories.

## When to use each

Use Occam's Razor when you have multiple plausible explanations and need to pick the most likely one. Use First Principles when all existing explanations seem inadequate and you need to build understanding from scratch.

## In practice

Barry Marshall used Occam's Razor when he proposed bacteria — not stress — caused ulcers. The simpler explanation fit the evidence better. SpaceX used First Principles to question why rockets cost what they do, ignoring existing price structures entirely.

## They work together

These models aren't competitors — they're complements. The best thinkers use both, selecting the right tool for the right situation. Occam's Razor and First Principles Thinking often appear together in [Model Chains](/learn/model-chains/), where the output of one feeds into the other.
