## Inversion

Instead of asking how to succeed, ask what would guarantee failure — then avoid it.

[Read the full model →](/models/inversion/)

## Pre-Mortem

Before starting, imagine the project has already failed. Then figure out why.

[Read the full model →](/models/pre-mortem/)

## What's the difference?

Both models examine failure, but from different angles. Inversion asks 'what would guarantee failure?' as a general thinking tool applicable to any goal. A Pre-Mortem is a specific technique applied to a concrete plan — imagining the project has already failed and working backwards to identify causes.

## When to use each

Use Inversion early in the thinking process to reframe a hard forward question into an easier backward one. Use a Pre-Mortem later, once you have a specific plan, to stress-test it before execution.

## In practice

Charlie Munger uses Inversion as a daily thinking habit across all decisions. Gary Klein developed the Pre-Mortem as a team exercise specifically for project planning, giving team members permission to voice concerns that groupthink would normally suppress.

## They work together

These models aren't competitors — they're complements. The best thinkers use both, selecting the right tool for the right situation. Inversion and Pre-Mortem often appear together in [Model Chains](/learn/model-chains/), where the output of one feeds into the other.
