## Probabilistic Thinking

Think in likelihoods, not certainties. Assign probabilities to outcomes instead of assuming binary results.

[Read the full model →](/models/probabilistic-thinking/)

## Bayesian Updating

Start with your best guess, then update it proportionally as new evidence arrives.

[Read the full model →](/models/bayesian-updating/)

## What's the difference?

Probabilistic Thinking is the broad practice of thinking in likelihoods rather than certainties. Bayesian Updating is the specific method for adjusting those likelihoods as new evidence arrives. Probabilistic Thinking is the philosophy. Bayesian Updating is the mechanics.

## When to use each

Use Probabilistic Thinking as your default mode for any uncertain situation — assigning probabilities rather than assuming binary outcomes. Use Bayesian Updating when new information arrives and you need to calibrate how much it should change your estimate.

## In practice

Philip Tetlock's superforecasters use both: Probabilistic Thinking to frame predictions as percentages, and Bayesian Updating to revise those percentages with each new data point. Alan Turing used Bayesian Updating at industrial scale to crack the Enigma code.

## They work together

These models aren't competitors — they're complements. The best thinkers use both, selecting the right tool for the right situation. Probabilistic Thinking and Bayesian Updating often appear together in [Model Chains](/learn/model-chains/), where the output of one feeds into the other.
