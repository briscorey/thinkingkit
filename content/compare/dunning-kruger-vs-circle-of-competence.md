## Dunning-Kruger Effect

People with low competence in a domain tend to overestimate their ability, while experts tend to underestimate theirs.

[Read the full model →](/models/dunning-kruger/)

## Circle of Competence

Know what you know, know what you don't know, and stay honest about the boundary.

[Read the full model →](/models/circle-of-competence/)

## What's the difference?

Dunning-Kruger describes the psychological phenomenon: people with low competence overestimate their abilities, while experts underestimate theirs. Circle of Competence is the strategic response: define your actual boundaries and operate accordingly. One is a bias to watch for; the other is a framework for action.

## When to use each

Use Dunning-Kruger awareness to calibrate confidence levels — especially your own. Use Circle of Competence to make decisions about where to invest effort and where to seek expert help.

## They work together

These models aren't competitors — they're complements. The best thinkers use both.
