## Network Effects

Some products become more valuable as more people use them — creating winner-take-all dynamics.

[Read the full model →](/models/network-effects/)

## Critical Mass

Some processes need a minimum threshold of input before anything happens — then they suddenly accelerate.

[Read the full model →](/models/critical-mass/)

## What's the difference?

Network Effects describe how a product becomes more valuable as more people use it. Critical Mass is the threshold at which the network effect becomes self-sustaining — where growth generates more growth without external push. Network effects are the mechanism; critical mass is the tipping point.

## When to use each

Use Network Effects thinking when evaluating platforms and marketplaces. Use Critical Mass thinking when planning launches — focus resources on reaching the tipping point in one segment before expanding.

## They work together

These models aren't competitors — they're complements. The best thinkers use both.
