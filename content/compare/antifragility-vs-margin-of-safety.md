## Antifragility

Some things don't just survive shocks — they get stronger from them. Position yourself to benefit from disorder.

## Margin of Safety

Build a buffer between what you expect and what you plan for. The world will surprise you.

## What's the difference?

Margin of Safety is defensive — it builds buffers to survive shocks. Antifragility goes further — it structures systems to actually benefit from shocks. A margin of safety prevents ruin. Antifragility converts volatility into growth.

## When to use each

Use Margin of Safety for situations where your goal is survival and stability. Use Antifragility when you want to design systems that improve under stress — where you can afford small losses for the chance of disproportionate gains.

## In practice

Benjamin Graham's value investing approach uses Margin of Safety — buy stocks below their intrinsic value so you have a buffer against being wrong. Nassim Taleb's barbell strategy uses Antifragility — combine ultra-safe assets with small speculative bets that pay off enormously in crises.
