## Antifragility

Some things don't just survive shocks — they get stronger from them. Position yourself to benefit from disorder.

[Read the full model →](/models/antifragility/)

## Optionality

Create situations with limited downside and unlimited upside. Keep your options open until you have to commit.

[Read the full model →](/models/optionality/)

## What's the difference?

Antifragility is the property of systems that gain from disorder. Optionality is the mechanism that often creates antifragility — having the right but not the obligation to act. Optionality is a tool; antifragility is the result of using that tool well.

## When to use each

Use Optionality thinking when structuring decisions — preserve the right to change direction. Use Antifragility thinking when designing entire systems — build them to gain from the volatility you can't predict.

## They work together

These models aren't competitors — they're complements. The best thinkers use both.
