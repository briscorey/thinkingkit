## Mimetic Desire

We don't want things independently — we want things because other people want them. Desire is borrowed, not original.

[Read the full model →](/models/mimetic-desire/)

## Bandwagon Effect

The tendency to adopt beliefs and behaviours simply because many other people do.

[Read the full model →](/models/bandwagon-effect/)

## What's the difference?

Mimetic Desire (Girard) says we borrow our desires from models we admire or compete with — we want things because specific people want them. The Bandwagon Effect says we adopt behaviours because many people are doing them — we follow the crowd. One is about imitating specific individuals; the other is about following masses.

## When to use each

Use Mimetic Desire awareness for personal decisions — am I wanting this because of who I'm imitating? Use Bandwagon awareness for collective phenomena — am I joining because everyone else is?

## They work together

These models aren't competitors — they're complements. The best thinkers use both.
