In a world of misinformation, hot takes, and motivated reasoning, the ability to evaluate arguments rigorously is a superpower. These models give you a systematic process for separating strong claims from weak ones.

<div style="max-width:680px;margin:var(--space-xl) auto;">

<div style="display:flex;gap:var(--space-lg);align-items:flex-start;margin-bottom:var(--space-xl);padding:var(--space-lg);background:var(--color-bg-card);border:1px solid var(--color-border-light);border-radius:var(--radius-lg);box-shadow:var(--shadow-sm);">
  <span style="font-family:var(--font-display);font-style:italic;font-size:1.8rem;color:var(--color-accent);opacity:0.4;min-width:36px;">1</span>
  <div>
    <h3 style="margin-top:0;"><a href="/models/steelmanning/">Steelmanning</a></h3>
    <p style="font-size:0.88rem;">Before critiquing an argument, rebuild the strongest possible version of it.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div style="display:flex;gap:var(--space-lg);align-items:flex-start;margin-bottom:var(--space-xl);padding:var(--space-lg);background:var(--color-bg-card);border:1px solid var(--color-border-light);border-radius:var(--radius-lg);box-shadow:var(--shadow-sm);">
  <span style="font-family:var(--font-display);font-style:italic;font-size:1.8rem;color:var(--color-accent);opacity:0.4;min-width:36px;">2</span>
  <div>
    <h3 style="margin-top:0;"><a href="/models/falsifiability/">Falsifiability</a></h3>
    <p style="font-size:0.88rem;">Check whether the claim can even be proven wrong in principle. If not, it's not saying much.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div style="display:flex;gap:var(--space-lg);align-items:flex-start;margin-bottom:var(--space-xl);padding:var(--space-lg);background:var(--color-bg-card);border:1px solid var(--color-border-light);border-radius:var(--radius-lg);box-shadow:var(--shadow-sm);">
  <span style="font-family:var(--font-display);font-style:italic;font-size:1.8rem;color:var(--color-accent);opacity:0.4;min-width:36px;">3</span>
  <div>
    <h3 style="margin-top:0;"><a href="/models/base-rate-neglect/">Base Rate Neglect</a></h3>
    <p style="font-size:0.88rem;">Before evaluating specific evidence, check how common the claimed outcome is in general.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div style="display:flex;gap:var(--space-lg);align-items:flex-start;margin-bottom:var(--space-xl);padding:var(--space-lg);background:var(--color-bg-card);border:1px solid var(--color-border-light);border-radius:var(--radius-lg);box-shadow:var(--shadow-sm);">
  <span style="font-family:var(--font-display);font-style:italic;font-size:1.8rem;color:var(--color-accent);opacity:0.4;min-width:36px;">4</span>
  <div>
    <h3 style="margin-top:0;"><a href="/models/bayesian-updating/">Bayesian Updating</a></h3>
    <p style="font-size:0.88rem;">Update your confidence proportionally to the strength of new evidence — neither too much nor too little.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div style="display:flex;gap:var(--space-lg);align-items:flex-start;margin-bottom:var(--space-xl);padding:var(--space-lg);background:var(--color-bg-card);border:1px solid var(--color-border-light);border-radius:var(--radius-lg);box-shadow:var(--shadow-sm);">
  <span style="font-family:var(--font-display);font-style:italic;font-size:1.8rem;color:var(--color-accent);opacity:0.4;min-width:36px;">5</span>
  <div>
    <h3 style="margin-top:0;"><a href="/models/confirmation-bias/">Confirmation Bias</a></h3>
    <p style="font-size:0.88rem;">Ask whether you're evaluating the evidence fairly or filtering it through your existing beliefs.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div style="display:flex;gap:var(--space-lg);align-items:flex-start;margin-bottom:var(--space-xl);padding:var(--space-lg);background:var(--color-bg-card);border:1px solid var(--color-border-light);border-radius:var(--radius-lg);box-shadow:var(--shadow-sm);">
  <span style="font-family:var(--font-display);font-style:italic;font-size:1.8rem;color:var(--color-accent);opacity:0.4;min-width:36px;">6</span>
  <div>
    <h3 style="margin-top:0;"><a href="/models/correlation-causation/">Correlation Causation</a></h3>
    <p style="font-size:0.88rem;">Just because two things happen together doesn't mean one causes the other.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div style="display:flex;gap:var(--space-lg);align-items:flex-start;margin-bottom:var(--space-xl);padding:var(--space-lg);background:var(--color-bg-card);border:1px solid var(--color-border-light);border-radius:var(--radius-lg);box-shadow:var(--shadow-sm);">
  <span style="font-family:var(--font-display);font-style:italic;font-size:1.8rem;color:var(--color-accent);opacity:0.4;min-width:36px;">7</span>
  <div>
    <h3 style="margin-top:0;"><a href="/models/narrative-fallacy/">Narrative Fallacy</a></h3>
    <p style="font-size:0.88rem;">Beware of compelling stories — the human need for narrative can make weak evidence feel conclusive.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div style="display:flex;gap:var(--space-lg);align-items:flex-start;margin-bottom:var(--space-xl);padding:var(--space-lg);background:var(--color-bg-card);border:1px solid var(--color-border-light);border-radius:var(--radius-lg);box-shadow:var(--shadow-sm);">
  <span style="font-family:var(--font-display);font-style:italic;font-size:1.8rem;color:var(--color-accent);opacity:0.4;min-width:36px;">8</span>
  <div>
    <h3 style="margin-top:0;"><a href="/models/reversion-incentives/">Reversion Incentives</a></h3>
    <p style="font-size:0.88rem;">Check who benefits from you believing this argument. Incentive structures predict conclusions.</p>
  </div>
</div>
