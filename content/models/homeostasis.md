## How it works

Homeostasis is a system's tendency to resist change and return to its equilibrium state. Your body maintains a core temperature of 37°C — when you're too hot, you sweat; when you're too cold, you shiver. The system actively resists deviations from its set point.

Organisations, markets, and social systems exhibit homeostasis too. A company's culture resists change even when leadership mandates it. A dieter's body resists weight loss by lowering metabolism. A team's performance reverts to its established norm after an unusually good or bad period.

## Case study: How the human body fights weight loss with a homeostatic vengeance

The biggest loser study, published in 2016, tracked contestants from the TV show six years after their dramatic weight losses. Almost all had regained most of the weight. More remarkably, their metabolic rates remained suppressed — their bodies were burning 500+ fewer calories per day than expected for their size, even six years later.

The body's homeostatic systems had interpreted rapid weight loss as starvation and permanently adjusted the metabolic set point downward. The contestants were fighting not just appetite but their own physiology. Homeostasis wasn't a temporary resistance — it was a persistent, active counterforce to the change they'd made.

## When to use it

Apply homeostasis awareness whenever you're trying to change a system that has an established equilibrium. Ask: "What mechanisms will actively resist this change and try to restore the previous state?"

## Common mistakes

The main mistake is fighting homeostasis with willpower rather than changing the set point. Sustainable change requires altering the equilibrium itself — the feedback loops that maintain the current state — not just pushing against it temporarily.

## Try it now

Think of a change you've tried to make — personal, organisational, or systemic — that reverted to the old state. What homeostatic mechanism pulled it back? What would it take to change the underlying set point rather than just pushing against it?
