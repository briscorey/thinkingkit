## How it works

Specialists develop deep expertise in a narrow domain. Generalists develop broad competence across many domains. The optimal strategy depends on environmental stability: in stable, predictable environments, specialists outperform (a narrow but deep advantage wins consistently). In volatile, unpredictable environments, generalists outperform (broad adaptability beats narrow expertise when conditions change).

David Epstein's research in *Range* showed that in "kind" learning environments with clear rules and fast feedback (chess, golf, classical music), early specialisation works. In "wicked" environments with ambiguous rules and delayed feedback (business, geopolitics, most real-world domains), generalists who sampled widely before committing outperformed early specialists.

## Case study: How Roger Federer's late specialisation produced the greatest tennis career

Against the prevailing wisdom of early specialisation, Roger Federer played multiple sports — basketball, handball, badminton, skiing, wrestling, swimming, and skateboarding — well into his teens. He didn't specialise in tennis until age 16, far later than most elite players.

Federer went on to win 20 Grand Slam titles and is widely considered the most complete player in tennis history. His multi-sport background gave him unusual movement patterns, hand-eye coordination from different angles, and tactical creativity that pure tennis specialists lacked. Late specialisation didn't handicap him — it gave him a broader foundation from which to build unmatched tennis ability.

## When to use it

Ask: "Is my environment stable and well-defined (specialise early) or volatile and ambiguous (stay broad longer)?" Most career environments are far more volatile than people assume, which argues for broader skill development than conventional wisdom suggests.

## Try it now

Map your current skills. Are you a deep specialist or a broad generalist? Given the volatility of your environment, is that the right balance? What adjacent skills might you develop to add resilience?
