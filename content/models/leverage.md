## How it works

Leverage means using a tool, system, or resource to amplify your output beyond what direct effort alone could produce. Naval Ravikant identifies four forms of leverage: labour (other people working for you), capital (money working for you), code (software working for you), and media (content working for you).

The last two — code and media — are what Naval calls "permissionless leverage." You don't need anyone's approval to write software or publish content. A single person with code and media leverage can have the impact of a thousand-person organisation.

## Case study: How one person at Craigslist replaced thousands of newspaper classified employees

Craig Newmark started Craigslist in 1995 as a simple email list of San Francisco events. By 2000, with a tiny team, Craigslist was processing more classified advertising than all U.S. newspapers combined. The platform generated billions in value with roughly 50 employees.

Meanwhile, newspaper classified departments employed tens of thousands of people to perform the same function less efficiently. Code leverage — software that runs 24/7, scales to millions of users, and costs almost nothing per additional transaction — replaced an entire industry's workforce. One developer's code had more leverage than thousands of classified sales representatives.

## When to use it

Apply leverage thinking whenever you're deciding how to invest your time. Ask: "Am I using direct effort when a lever could multiply my impact? Could code, media, capital, or other people's efforts amplify this?"

## Common mistakes

The biggest mistake is pursuing leverage without foundational skills. Leverage amplifies whatever it's applied to — including incompetence. The second mistake is ignoring the costs of leverage: managing people creates overhead, capital creates risk, code requires maintenance, and media requires consistency.

## Try it now

Audit your current work. What percentage of your output comes from direct personal effort versus leveraged systems (code, delegation, content, capital)? Where could you add leverage to multiply your impact without proportionally increasing your time?
