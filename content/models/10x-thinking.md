## How it works

10x thinking, championed by Google's Astro Teller, rejects incremental improvement. Instead of asking "how do I make this 10% better?", you ask "how do I make this 10 times better?" The radical constraint forces you to abandon the existing approach entirely and search for breakthrough solutions.

A 10% improvement lets you keep the current system and optimise within it. A 10x improvement requires you to rethink the fundamental approach. This is why 10x goals can actually be easier than 10% goals — they liberate you from the constraints of the existing paradigm.

## Case study: How Google X's Loon project brought internet to remote areas

Google needed to provide internet access to the 4 billion people without it. A 10% improvement would mean building more cell towers — expensive and slow. The 10x question was: "What if we put cell towers in the sky?"

Project Loon launched internet-broadcasting balloons into the stratosphere, using wind currents to navigate them into position. A single balloon could cover an area of 5,000 square kilometres. The approach was completely impractical by conventional telecom standards — which is exactly why no one had tried it. The 10x framing made a seemingly crazy idea the obvious answer.

## When to use it

Use 10x thinking when incremental improvement isn't producing meaningful results, when you're stuck optimising a fundamentally limited approach, or when the problem is important enough to justify rethinking from scratch.

## Common mistakes

The main mistake is applying 10x thinking to everything. Not every problem needs a moonshot — sometimes 10% improvement is the right goal. The second mistake is using 10x as an excuse for vaporware: setting impossible goals without a plan to achieve them. 10x thinking should lead to unconventional action, not just unconventional aspiration.

## Try it now

Take your most important project. You've been improving it incrementally. Now ask: what would it look like if it were 10 times better? Not 10% — ten times. What would you have to completely change about your approach?
