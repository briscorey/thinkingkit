## How it works

A power law distribution is one where a small number of items account for a disproportionate share of the total. Unlike a normal (bell curve) distribution where most values cluster around the average, power law distributions have fat tails — a few extreme values dominate.

This appears everywhere: a tiny fraction of earthquakes cause the vast majority of damage, a few authors sell most of the books, a small number of venture investments produce nearly all the returns, and a handful of your habits drive most of your outcomes.

The practical implication: in power law domains, averages are misleading, and concentrating effort on the vital few produces far more value than spreading it across the trivial many.

## Real-world examples

**Venture capital.** Returns follow a power law so extreme that a single company (like Google or Facebook) can return more than the rest of a fund combined. This is why VCs optimise for finding the outlier, not avoiding failures.

**Content creation.** A few posts, videos, or songs generate almost all the engagement. Creators who understand this focus on maximising the ceiling of their best work rather than raising the floor of their worst.

## When to use it

Apply power law thinking whenever you're allocating resources. Ask: "Is this a power law domain or a normal distribution domain?" In power law domains, concentrating effort on the best opportunities beats diversifying evenly.

## Common mistakes

The main mistake is assuming everything follows a power law. Human height, test scores, and manufacturing tolerances follow normal distributions — the average is meaningful and extremes are rare. The second mistake is using power law thinking to justify ignoring fundamentals. Even in power law domains, you need a baseline of quality across the board.

## Try it now

Look at any area where you track results — sales, content performance, customer acquisition, personal productivity. What percentage of your inputs produce 80% of your outputs? If you could double down on just those top performers, what would change?
