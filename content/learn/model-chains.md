Individual mental models are useful. Chaining them together is transformative. Each chain below shows a sequence of 3–5 models applied in order to a specific type of challenge — with the output of each step feeding into the next.

## The Risk Assessment Chain

*Before committing to anything important, run it through this sequence.*

<div style="position:relative;padding-left:32px;border-left:2px solid var(--color-accent);margin:var(--space-lg) 0 var(--space-2xl);">
  <div style="margin-bottom:var(--space-xl);position:relative;">
    <div style="position:absolute;left:-41px;top:0;width:20px;height:20px;border-radius:50%;background:var(--color-accent);display:flex;align-items:center;justify-content:center;color:#fff;font-size:0.65rem;font-weight:700;">1</div>
    <h4 style="margin-top:0;"><a href="/models/pre-mortem/">Pre-Mortem</a></h4>
    <p style="font-size:0.88rem;">Imagine the project has already failed. List every reason why. This surfaces risks your optimism hides.</p>
    <p style="font-size:0.78rem;color:var(--color-accent);margin:0;">Output → A list of specific failure modes</p>
  </div>
  <div style="margin-bottom:var(--space-xl);position:relative;">
    <div style="position:absolute;left:-41px;top:0;width:20px;height:20px;border-radius:50%;background:var(--color-accent);display:flex;align-items:center;justify-content:center;color:#fff;font-size:0.65rem;font-weight:700;">2</div>
    <h4 style="margin-top:0;"><a href="/models/inversion/">Inversion</a></h4>
    <p style="font-size:0.88rem;">For each failure mode, invert it into a prevention action. What specifically would you do to make each failure impossible?</p>
    <p style="font-size:0.78rem;color:var(--color-accent);margin:0;">Output → A prevention plan for each risk</p>
  </div>
  <div style="margin-bottom:var(--space-xl);position:relative;">
    <div style="position:absolute;left:-41px;top:0;width:20px;height:20px;border-radius:50%;background:var(--color-accent);display:flex;align-items:center;justify-content:center;color:#fff;font-size:0.65rem;font-weight:700;">3</div>
    <h4 style="margin-top:0;"><a href="/models/antifragility/">Antifragility</a></h4>
    <p style="font-size:0.88rem;">Can you restructure so that some of these risks actually benefit you if they occur? Look for barbell opportunities — cap the downside, open the upside.</p>
    <p style="font-size:0.78rem;color:var(--color-accent);margin:0;">Output → A plan that gains from some volatility</p>
  </div>
  <div style="position:relative;">
    <div style="position:absolute;left:-41px;top:0;width:20px;height:20px;border-radius:50%;background:var(--color-accent);display:flex;align-items:center;justify-content:center;color:#fff;font-size:0.65rem;font-weight:700;">4</div>
    <h4 style="margin-top:0;"><a href="/models/margin-of-safety/">Margin of Safety</a></h4>
    <p style="font-size:0.88rem;">Add buffer. Whatever your timeline, budget, and resource estimates are — add margin. You've already identified the risks. Now insure against the ones you missed.</p>
    <p style="font-size:0.78rem;color:var(--color-teal);margin:0;">Result → A robust, antifragile plan with known risks mitigated</p>
  </div>
</div>

## The Decision Quality Chain

*For high-stakes decisions where you want to be thorough.*

<div style="position:relative;padding-left:32px;border-left:2px solid var(--color-accent);margin:var(--space-lg) 0 var(--space-2xl);">
  <div style="margin-bottom:var(--space-xl);position:relative;">
    <div style="position:absolute;left:-41px;top:0;width:20px;height:20px;border-radius:50%;background:var(--color-accent);display:flex;align-items:center;justify-content:center;color:#fff;font-size:0.65rem;font-weight:700;">1</div>
    <h4 style="margin-top:0;"><a href="/models/first-principles/">First Principles</a></h4>
    <p style="font-size:0.88rem;">Strip away assumptions. What's actually true about this situation? What are you accepting as given that might not be?</p>
    <p style="font-size:0.78rem;color:var(--color-accent);margin:0;">Output → The fundamental truths of the situation</p>
  </div>
  <div style="margin-bottom:var(--space-xl);position:relative;">
    <div style="position:absolute;left:-41px;top:0;width:20px;height:20px;border-radius:50%;background:var(--color-accent);display:flex;align-items:center;justify-content:center;color:#fff;font-size:0.65rem;font-weight:700;">2</div>
    <h4 style="margin-top:0;"><a href="/models/second-order-thinking/">Second-Order Thinking</a></h4>
    <p style="font-size:0.88rem;">For each option, map the consequences — then the consequences of those consequences. What happens after "what happens next"?</p>
    <p style="font-size:0.78rem;color:var(--color-accent);margin:0;">Output → A map of cascading effects for each option</p>
  </div>
  <div style="margin-bottom:var(--space-xl);position:relative;">
    <div style="position:absolute;left:-41px;top:0;width:20px;height:20px;border-radius:50%;background:var(--color-accent);display:flex;align-items:center;justify-content:center;color:#fff;font-size:0.65rem;font-weight:700;">3</div>
    <h4 style="margin-top:0;"><a href="/models/opportunity-cost/">Opportunity Cost</a></h4>
    <p style="font-size:0.88rem;">For the leading option, identify the best alternative you're giving up. Is the gap between them large enough to justify the choice?</p>
    <p style="font-size:0.78rem;color:var(--color-accent);margin:0;">Output → The true cost of your preferred option</p>
  </div>
  <div style="margin-bottom:var(--space-xl);position:relative;">
    <div style="position:absolute;left:-41px;top:0;width:20px;height:20px;border-radius:50%;background:var(--color-accent);display:flex;align-items:center;justify-content:center;color:#fff;font-size:0.65rem;font-weight:700;">4</div>
    <h4 style="margin-top:0;"><a href="/models/probabilistic-thinking/">Probabilistic Thinking</a></h4>
    <p style="font-size:0.88rem;">Assign probabilities to each outcome. Calculate expected values. Does the option with the best expected value match your intuitive preference?</p>
    <p style="font-size:0.78rem;color:var(--color-accent);margin:0;">Output → A probability-weighted comparison</p>
  </div>
  <div style="position:relative;">
    <div style="position:absolute;left:-41px;top:0;width:20px;height:20px;border-radius:50%;background:var(--color-accent);display:flex;align-items:center;justify-content:center;color:#fff;font-size:0.65rem;font-weight:700;">5</div>
    <h4 style="margin-top:0;"><a href="/models/regret-minimization/">Regret Minimization</a></h4>
    <p style="font-size:0.88rem;">Project yourself to age 80. Which choice will you regret not taking? If the analysis and the gut diverge, pay attention to both.</p>
    <p style="font-size:0.78rem;color:var(--color-teal);margin:0;">Result → A high-quality decision you can stand behind</p>
  </div>
</div>

## The Argument Evaluation Chain

*Before accepting or rejecting any claim.*

<div style="position:relative;padding-left:32px;border-left:2px solid var(--color-accent);margin:var(--space-lg) 0 var(--space-2xl);">
  <div style="margin-bottom:var(--space-xl);position:relative;">
    <div style="position:absolute;left:-41px;top:0;width:20px;height:20px;border-radius:50%;background:var(--color-accent);display:flex;align-items:center;justify-content:center;color:#fff;font-size:0.65rem;font-weight:700;">1</div>
    <h4 style="margin-top:0;"><a href="/models/steelmanning/">Steelmanning</a></h4>
    <p style="font-size:0.88rem;">Rebuild the strongest possible version of the argument. If you can't make a compelling case for it, you don't understand it well enough to reject it.</p>
  </div>
  <div style="margin-bottom:var(--space-xl);position:relative;">
    <div style="position:absolute;left:-41px;top:0;width:20px;height:20px;border-radius:50%;background:var(--color-accent);display:flex;align-items:center;justify-content:center;color:#fff;font-size:0.65rem;font-weight:700;">2</div>
    <h4 style="margin-top:0;"><a href="/models/base-rate-neglect/">Base Rate Check</a></h4>
    <p style="font-size:0.88rem;">How common is this claim's conclusion in general? What does the background data say before you consider the specific evidence?</p>
  </div>
  <div style="margin-bottom:var(--space-xl);position:relative;">
    <div style="position:absolute;left:-41px;top:0;width:20px;height:20px;border-radius:50%;background:var(--color-accent);display:flex;align-items:center;justify-content:center;color:#fff;font-size:0.65rem;font-weight:700;">3</div>
    <h4 style="margin-top:0;"><a href="/models/bayesian-updating/">Bayesian Updating</a></h4>
    <p style="font-size:0.88rem;">How much should this evidence shift your prior belief? Update proportionally — strong evidence moves you a lot, weak evidence moves you a little.</p>
  </div>
  <div style="position:relative;">
    <div style="position:absolute;left:-41px;top:0;width:20px;height:20px;border-radius:50%;background:var(--color-accent);display:flex;align-items:center;justify-content:center;color:#fff;font-size:0.65rem;font-weight:700;">4</div>
    <h4 style="margin-top:0;"><a href="/models/falsifiability/">Falsifiability Check</a></h4>
    <p style="font-size:0.88rem;">Could any evidence, even in principle, prove this claim wrong? If not, the claim is unfalsifiable — it feels like knowledge but it isn't.</p>
    <p style="font-size:0.78rem;color:var(--color-teal);margin:0;">Result → A calibrated, honest assessment of the claim's strength</p>
  </div>
</div>

## Build your own chains

These chains are starting points. As you learn more models, you'll discover your own powerful combinations. The skill is recognising which models apply to your specific situation and in what order.

Use the [Thinking Toolkit Matcher](/tools/toolkit-matcher/) to find models for your situation, then sequence them into your own chain.
